POP3 and SMTP

Post Office Protocol Version 3

Post Office Protocol (POP3) helps manage email traffic similarly to IMAP and is considered its sister protocol. Unlike IMAP, POP3 downloads emails to the local drive from the mail server after the connection is established. Users can configure email clients to check messages at set intervals. However, POP3 lacks real-time synchronization, which is considered a preferred feature among consumers.

Simple Mail Transfer Protocol

SMTP, or Simple Mail Transfer Protocol, works alongside POP3 and IMAP to send email. SMTP ensures end-to-end delivery between email clients. Messages are sent from the sender’s client to the recipient’s client, completing the client-to-client communication. This is achieved using the TCP/IP protocol stack, which all three protocols utilize to effectively transfer messages over a mail server.
